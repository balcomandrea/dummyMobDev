GABON: AN ANDROID PERVASIVE GAME OF EXPLORATION USING REAL-TIME GEOGRAPHICAL POSITIONING SYSTEM TRACKING

Joanna P. Bacoco, Nikki P. Literatus, Wilfred L. Morado,
Maureen M. Villamor


ABSTRACT

  Video gaming provides the users with a form of interactive entertainment, a past-time and leisure activity that made billions of dollars as an industry. Since it was introduced, its definition has evolved, from an early Cathode ray tube amusement to the modern-day smartphones. Video gaming on smartphones is often called mobile gaming. Mobile gaming is appreciated by a lot of users round the world because of its convenience and portability.

  Creating a game with the hope of solving the crisis relating not only to game addiction but also relates to the cognitive and psychological functions of the player were the goals of the project. Pervasive games often have educational goals, aiming at combining learning with fun. By role playing, pervasive games encourage learning and they re able to stimulate students and engage them in learning activities by requiring different skills to be deployed simultaneously. Gabon is an Android mobile game that features a new and exciting gaming experience by traveling and unlocking pins set around the city of Davao.

1 of 2

  Gabon is a by-product of combining pervasive gaming to location-enabled mobile devices that allows players to experience both real and virtual world at the same time. The game will be developed with the use of Global Positioning System (GPS) as navigation system to locate the player, Google Maps API Version 2 with Hybrid View, and Facebook SDK for the integration of the game to the player’s Facebook account. With a smartphone, Gabon can be played anytime you want while enjoying your travel around Davao City.
